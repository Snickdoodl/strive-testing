Strengths & Weaknesses in Guilty Gear Strive

Many people playing Guilty Gear Strive often complain about the characters their opponents play, but they very rarely know what makes them strong or how to beat them. This leads to disorganized, pointless arguing over certain characters and moves. This website strives to clear the futility from these discussions and show what exactly makes certain characters strong, and how to beat them.

Sol Badguy

Pros

  • Fast buttons
    Sol has many fast pokes to extend his pressure and dominate the neutral. His non-commital 6s and his plus-on-block f.s give him the buttons he needs to easily win.
  • Block pressure
    Sol has a variety of plus-on-block moves that bolster his pressure. Both his c.s and f.s, and can be even further improved by gatling them into his overheads and lows. His 41236h can also lock you down and open you up to tick throws.
  • Incredible defense
    Sol has many great defensive options. His 3 frame 5k can be used abare in multiple situations to break out of pressure. In addition, he has 3 invincible reversals, two of which require no meter.

Cons

N/A


Potemkin

Pros

  • Massive pokes
    Potemkin's main pokes, f.s and 5h, have incredible range and can keep out unsuspecting opponents.
  • Strike/Throw mixups
    Potemkin has frightening 50/50 mixups with his Potemkin Buster, leading to high damage combos.

Cons

  • Terrible movement
    Potemkin has no dash or air dash. In addition, his main approach tool, [4]6H, is highly punishable by most characters.
  • Zoning issues
    Because of his terrible movement, he often struggles with zoning. He has 63214s. to deal with this, but it only works on projectiles.

Goldlewis Dickinson

Pros

  • High damage
    Goldlewis has incredible damage, being able to easily ToD most characters at high risk. In addition, his Behemoth Typhoons provide a large amount of chip, even when your opponent is blocking.
  • Block Pressure
    Goldlewis has a plethora of plus on block moves. On top of that, many of his Behmoth Typhoons guard crush. Use this in combination with the fact that the Typhoons do massive amounts of chip to get formidable offense against a blocking opponent.
  • Strong Okizeme
    Goldlewis has many ways of scoring a hard knockdown, and can use them to set up 214s or counter-hit the opponent with a meaty 6h or 63214h.

Cons

  • Slow frame data
    Goldlewis has very slow moves, with his fastest being his 6 frame 2p. Because of this, he lacks abare options, leading to your pressure being safer.
  • Slow movement
    Goldlewis has one of the slowest runs in the game, paired with an equally slow air dash. He also lacks unique movement options, which makes Goldlewis susceptible to zoning.
  • Zoning issues
    Goldlewis lacks moves with a large range. His two main anti-zoning tools, 214s and 236s, are meter reliant and highly punishable.

In conclusion, there are many reasons why certain characters are stronger than others. Only by looking at these reasons can we make a plan to lab and improve against certain playsyles.
